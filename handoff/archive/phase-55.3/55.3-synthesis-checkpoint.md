## 2. Strategic chapter — finetune vs features

*(Research gate: 7 sources read in full + recency scan — `research_brief_55.3.md`; envelope reproduced in live_check_55.3.md. Key findings cited as KF1-KF6 per the brief.)*

### 2.1 What the away week actually was

Reconciled primary data (55.1): the fund entered the week +21.9% and exited +19.2% — **the away week itself lost 2.26% while passive SPY gained 2.49%**. The +19% level is the April momentum book converting unrealized→realized (DELL +$1,105, SNDK +$541, MU +$399 — all April entries); the week's own decisions (10 within-week round trips) netted **−$132** against ~**$1** of metered compute. Attribution at n=7 is low-power by construction, but the point estimates say: β_SPY≈1.04, the 06-05 loss day was a broad semis selloff (SOXX −10.4%), and residual alpha is statistically indistinguishable from zero. **The away week earned regime returns minus churn drag, with no evidence of selection skill at this horizon.**

This is precisely what the 2025-2026 evaluation literature predicts (KF1): FINSABER (arXiv:2505.07078v5) — short-window LLM-trading wins "vanish under broader and longer evaluations" and buy-and-hold "significantly outperforms" over robust setups; StockBench (arXiv:2510.02209) — most LLM agents lose to passive B&H *even with zero modeled costs*. pyfinagent's away week is a clean in-house replication of the published result.

### 2.2 Statistical power — the MinTRL numbers (stated, as required)

Using Bailey-López de Prado's exact MinTRL with the observed away-week moments (skew −1.05, kurtosis 3.43; 95% one-sided vs SR\*=0):

- **At the observed away-week Sharpe magnitude: ≈377 daily observations** (55.1's stated figure; the brief's recompute gives ~450 under a stricter convention — same verdict). We have **7** (35 since inception).
- **At the backtest Sharpe 1.17** (optimizer_best.json, the most optimistic defensible assumption): **≈539 trading days ≈ 2.1 years** of live dailies.
- At a decay-haircut SR 0.5: ≈2,820 days ≈ **11 years** — effectively unprovable live.

Conclusion the operator must price: **no 1-2 week live window can establish skill** — it is 2 orders of magnitude short of MinTRL even under the friendliest assumption. The on-screen Sharpe 4.72 / PSR 0.9993 are sub-MinTRL noise; metrics-v2's **DSR=0.0 is the honest summary**. The live window's real value is a **sanity/stress gate** (per arXiv:2602.14233's structural-validity framing: sub-threshold windows support stress-test interpretations only, never deployable-alpha claims): does the fixed P&L readout hold up, does the kill switch arm on a real anchor, do cycles run clean, do non-HOLD trades fire — i.e. it closes *operational* DoD criteria, not statistical ones.

### 2.3 Agent-skill ROI — what the compute actually bought

The away week ran the lite 4-agent chain at ~$0.01/ticker with the conviction overlay dead (static `10.00` fallback) and the enrichment skills silent (F-19). The burn-vs-P&L reconciliation (55.2): **~$1 of metered compute against −$132 of churn** — Burn is not the N\* problem; Risk/churn is. Would full mode (debate, devil's-advocate, risk-assessment; measured $1-3/cycle/day) have helped? The literature's adversarial answer (KF3, KTD-Fin arXiv:2605.28359, Jun 2026): **adding capabilities/tools did not improve returns** — open-research mode performed *worse* than memory-only; 9/10 frontier LLMs showed negative selection alpha; headline returns were passive factor exposure. And pyfinagent's own data agrees: the Trader/RiskJudge rationales were *good* — they flagged parabolic blow-off risk, corrupted KR market caps, sector concentration — **and the architecture ignored them** (F-3: REJECT executed 3 times; F-7: fallback conviction 10.00; F-5: failed scores published as confident HOLDs). The bottleneck is not reasoning quality or reasoning quantity; it is that **the decision layer does not consume the reasoning it already pays for**.

### 2.5 Conclusion: FEATURES — specifically reliability features — with one measured churn lever as the alternative

**Recommendation: FEATURE** (the binding-RiskJudge variant below), for explicit reasons:

1. **The finding mass is feature-shaped.** Of the 19 consolidated findings, one (F-18 churn) is lever-shaped; the HIGH-severity cluster (F-3/F-4/F-5/F-6/F-7/F-8) is reliability/correctness-shaped — gates that don't bind, fallbacks that fail-confident, rails without health checks, telemetry that can't answer "what fired".
2. **The lever family has an adverse precedent on this book.** 53.1 measured the obvious churn lever (rank-hysteresis band) and honestly REJECTED it (ΔSharpe +0.015 < +0.05 floor, p=0.376, CI_low<0). Score-hysteresis is the same mechanism family renamed (auto-FAIL per the goal); the one mechanistically distinct lever with literature support (minimum holding period — KF2's named guardrail, KF4/KF7 net-of-cost support) is worth exactly one measured shot, and may also land within noise — 53.1's outcome warns us.
3. **The literature warns against analytical features and supports reliability features.** KF3 (adversarial): more agents/tools ≠ returns. KF5: the structural-validity gate's failing components map 1:1 onto F-3/F-5/F-7 (Rationale Robustness, Epistemic Calibration) — fixing those is what converts a stress-test system into a candidate for deployable-alpha claims later.
4. **Full-mode-agents-in-the-autonomous-path is explicitly NOT the recommended FEATURE**: it buys richer rationale (operator trust, auditability) at $1-3/day but the evidence (KF3 + the away week's own ignored-rationale pattern) does not support it as a profit mechanism while F-3/F-7 stand. Fix consumption before buying more production.

### 2.6 Phase-57 candidate specs (one paragraph each; full payload authored at install for the CHOSEN variant only)

**LEVER — Minimum holding period (anti-whipsaw time-gate).** Add a config-gated, default-OFF `paper_min_holding_days` (sweep 2/3/5 cycles) that blocks churn/swap-reason SELLs of a held paper position before it has been held N cycles, while ALWAYS letting risk exits (stop-loss, trailing stop, kill switch) override — so risk control is never weakened (do-no-harm). This is the time-axis guardrail the literature names for LLM churn ("without system-level guardrails like minimum holding periods or cooldown windows, the LLM's linguistic uncertainty is directly executed as trades" — arXiv:2505.07078v5) and is **mechanistically distinct from the 53.1-rejected rank-hysteresis band** (caps time-in-position, not name-set rank; score-hysteresis was considered and REJECTED here as same-family). Measure ON-vs-OFF via the $0 replay on the production S&P-500 universe (the 53.1 machinery), reporting Sharpe/return/turnover/maxDD gross AND net-of-cost, and promote ONLY on the same Ledoit-Wolf SR-difference gate as 52.3/53.1 (`analytics.sharpe_diff_test`, n_boot=5000, seed=42: `p_one_sided<0.05 AND delta>=+0.05 AND ci_low>0` net, gross `ci_low>-0.05`); a within-noise result is an honest REJECT and a valid outcome. US momentum core byte-identical with the flag OFF (unit-tested OFF-identity). Cites F-18; KF2/KF4/KF7.

**FEATURE — Binding RiskJudge gate + concentration-aware sizing (make the risk verdict drive behavior).** Convert RiskJudge REJECT from advisory (F-3: three away-week REJECTs executed, incl. DELL 06-03) into a config-gated, default-OFF **binding** gate that blocks the BUY when the judge returns REJECT, and inject the live portfolio sector breakdown + the real configured caps into the judge's prompt/context so it stops reasoning blind (F-8: phantom "10% cap" vs config 30%, "no portfolio sector breakdown was provided"). Acceptance criteria of 53.1 rigor: a regression fixture where a REJECT candidate executes on pre-fix code and is blocked with the flag ON; a replay of the away week quantifying the would-have-been-blocked trades and their realized P&L (the three REJECT trades are the natural event study); prompt-context test asserting the judge receives sector weights + the 30% figure; default-OFF, US momentum core byte-identical, NO live flag flip inside phase-57. Highest-EV capability gap per KF3 (reliability > analytical) and KF5 (Rationale-Robustness/Epistemic-Calibration components). Cites F-3/F-8/B11; KF3/KF5. *(Considered and ranked lower: per-market benchmark fetch ^KS11 (F-12 — cosmetic vs risk); full-mode agents in the autonomous path (KF3-adverse, §2.5.4).)*
