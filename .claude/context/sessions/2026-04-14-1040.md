## Operational observations worth preserving

### Subagent reliability is environment-dependent

Within ~30 minutes of each other, two parallel Ford sessions spawned qa-evaluator subagents on the same task:

| Session | Subagent path | Outcome |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| `01N2kVnV3saBvEMX6Md8MaLS` (mine) | researcher AND qa-evaluator | Both `Stream idle timeout - partial response received` |
| `01Tz9Qbs7tLppqNLucEpgVs3` (parallel) | qa-evaluator (general-purpose, Opus, anti-leniency) | Clean PASS, full critique returned |

Both sessions are remote, both are on Opus 4.6, both target the same commit. The failure is non-deterministic. Working hypothesis: stream-idle-timeout fires when the subagent goes too long between stdout writes, which is more likely on long sequential WebSearch chains than on AST-only QA verification. The parallel session's qa-evaluator was AST-only (no web), and that's likely why it succeeded where my web-heavy researcher path failed.

**Suggestion for Peder:** the qa-evaluator subagent is reliable enough to use — the failures concentrate on the researcher path. Consider either (a) splitting researcher work into smaller chunks, (b) configuring the researcher subagent to flush stdout more aggressively, or (c) accepting the in-session WebSearch fallback for research and reserving subagents for QA-only work.

### Race-condition pattern

Two parallel remote sessions independently picked up the same orphaned step from masterplan.json. There's no locking. The first to push wins; the second has to reset. Soft suggestion: a session-start hook that checks `git fetch origin && grep -l "Phase X" $(git log --since='1 hour' --name-only --pretty=)` before claiming a step would prevent this. Not blocking.
