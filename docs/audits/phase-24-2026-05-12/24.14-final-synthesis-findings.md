# Findings — phase-24.14 — Final Synthesis + Ranked Phase-25.x Candidate List

## Executive summary

Phase-24's 14 audit buckets (24.0-24.13) produced **45 distinct phase-25.x candidates** after deduplication across overlapping cross-bucket references. The ranked list groups candidates by impact + effort: **8 P0 candidates** (live financial / operational — ship immediately), **19 P1 candidates** (critical mechanism absent or significant quality gap), and **18 P2 candidates** (nice-to-have improvements). Dependency-ordering overrides raw WSJF score in 6 cases: e.g., 25.A9 (cost-tracker cache fix) precedes 25.A8 (cost-budget hard-block) since hard-block needs accurate cost data; 25.A3 (promoted_strategies BQ table) precedes 25.B3 (daily-loop poll) precedes 25.R (strategy auto-switch policy); 25.A (decouple RiskJudge) precedes 25.B (remove cosmetic patch). The most user-impactful P0 cluster (stops) closes the TER -$1,107 accruing loss: 25.1 (wire `check_stop_losses`) + 25.2 (backfill missing stops) + 25.6 (no-stop-on-entry hard block) can ship together. The second P0 cluster (Slack notifications) closes operator-visible bugs: 25.G (digest P&L double bug) + 25.H (5x SNDK dedup) + 25.J (trade confirmations) + 25.K (kill-switch Slack). The third P0 (cost discipline) is 25.A8 cost-budget hard-block + 25.A9 cache-write premium fix. Together these 8 P0 candidates address every user-reported bug from the 2026-05-12 operator report. P1 candidates address the red-line goal-c (strategy switching — 25.R + 25.A3/B3/C3/E3/F3) and goal-d (observability — 25.Q `profit_per_llm_dollar`, 25.S attribution, 25.A7 per-table freshness). P2 candidates address LLM cost optimization (25.B9/C9/D9/E9/F9 caching + batch + files + citations), frontend polish (25.A11/B11/C11, 25.A12/B12/C12), and MCP infrastructure (25.A10/B10/C10/D10). The total estimated effort: ~8 weeks of P0 work, ~16 weeks P1, ~12 weeks P2. Recommend a phase-25.0 charter step to establish ordering then individual phase-25.N implementation cycles per candidate.
